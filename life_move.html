MOVE

Learning through motion.

Movement, for me, is not escape but alignment —
between body, environment, and attention.
Each practice teaches a different way of inhabiting the world.

Hiking

Becoming part of the landscape.

Hiking dissolves the boundary between self and terrain. Step by step, attention shifts outward, aligning breath and rhythm with the mountains — a quiet practice of Daoist unity rather than conquest.

Golf

Reading time through seasons.

Golf slows perception. Across changing light, wind, and seasons, the game becomes a dialogue with variation. It is a shared journey—teaching patience, adjustment, and the joy of mutual encouragement and connection on the course.

Racing Car

Releasing controlled wildness.

On the track, instinct and precision converge. Racing offers a sanctioned space for speed, risk, and intensity — where restraint and wildness are held in balance rather than opposition.

Snowboarding

Learning resilience through repetition.

Snowboarding measures progress in falls as much as in runs. Each descent tests endurance and resolve, reinforcing the discipline of getting up — again and again — in thinner air and colder silence.

Travel

Expanding the radius of understanding.

Travel widens perception beyond familiarity. By encountering different landscapes, customs, and social rhythms, movement becomes education — cultivating perspective, empathy, and practical wisdom.
